Cookie Policy

Last updated: 30 June 2026

Cookies are small files a website can store on your device. They can also include similar technologies like local storage. This page explains what this website (steadyhand.app) does, which is very little.

1. Does this Site use cookies?

We do not set advertising cookies, and we do not track you across other sites. On your first visit the Site shows a consent banner. Only if you choose “Accept” do we load a privacy-friendly analytics tool (Plausible) to count visits. Plausible is cookieless: it sets no cookies, does not collect personal data, and does not build a profile of you. If you choose “Reject”, no analytics loads. You can change your choice at any time via “Cookie settings” in the footer.

2. Third-party requests

To display correctly, the Site loads a few resources from third parties. These do not track you for advertising, but you should be aware of them:

ProviderWhy it loadsWhat it can involve
Plausible AnalyticsAggregate visit counts, only after you acceptCookieless. Counts page views without cookies, personal data, or profiles. Loads only with your consent.
Hosting / CDN (e.g. GitHub or Cloudflare)Serving and securing the SiteStandard server logs and, in some cases, an essential security cookie set by the provider.
CalendlyOnly if you open the demo bookingCalendly may set its own cookies on its booking page. See Calendly’s cookie notice.

Our fonts and stylesheets are self-hosted and served from our own domain, so no third-party font or styling requests are made from your browser.

3. How to control cookies

You can block or delete cookies through your browser settings, and most browsers let you refuse third-party cookies. Doing so will not break this Site. For help, see your browser’s documentation or aboutcookies.org.

4. Changes

We will update this page if our use of cookies changes. The date at the top shows the latest version.
